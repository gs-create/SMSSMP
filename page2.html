Area of a Parallelogram: P4 Maths Fun!

What is a Parallelogram?

It's a four-sided shape where opposite sides are parallel. We need the **Base (b)** and the **Height (h)** (the straight up-and-down distance!) to find the area.

Why is the Area $A = b \times h$?

Watch how we can turn a parallelogram into a rectangle!

The Formula: **Area = Base √ó Height**

Step-by-Step Examples

Example 1:

Base = 10 cm, Height = 5 cm.

Area = 10 cm $\times$ 5 cm = **50 $\text{cm}^2$**

Example 2 (Watch Out!):

Base = 6 m, Slanted Side = 5 m, Height = 4 m.

We IGNORE the slanted side! Area = 6 m $\times$ 4 m = **24 $\text{m}^2$**
